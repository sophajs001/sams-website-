Ordination Ceremony

Published March 2026

Celebrating the ordination of our seminarians into priesthood. The ceremony was marked by prayer, joy, and the presence of family and clergy.

The ordination marks a significant milestone in the journey of faith and service, inspiring future seminarians to answer the call.

On March 15, 2026, the seminary community gathered in solemn joy for the long‑awaited Ordination Ceremony. The chapel was filled with family, clergy, and faithful supporters, all united to witness seminarians take their vows and step into priestly ministry. The day began with a procession of candidates, their faces radiant with anticipation and devotion. Hymns of praise echoed through the hall, setting a tone of reverence and celebration. The ordaining bishop spoke passionately about the sacred responsibility of priesthood, reminding the newly ordained that their mission is not only to serve the Church but to be living witnesses of Christ’s love in society. This ordination marks a significant milestone in the journey of faith and service. It inspires future seminarians to answer the call with courage, humility, and dedication. The ceremony also reaffirms the seminary’s mission of forming leaders who will guide the Church with wisdom, compassion, and unwavering commitment. As the newly ordained priests offered their first blessings, the atmosphere was charged with emotion — tears of joy, heartfelt prayers, and a renewed sense of hope for the future. The ordination day will be remembered not only as a personal triumph for the candidates but as a communal celebration of faith, service, and the enduring spirit of vocation.
